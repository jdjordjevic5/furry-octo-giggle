The period from 1660 to 1689 was marked as Restoration Literature or English Restoration at the time of Stuart period. English Restoration refers to homogeneous styles of literature on the basis of a celebration or reaction to the renewed court of Charles II. This literature involve extreme terms because it features both Paradise Lost and the Earl of Rochester Sodom, sexual comedy with his wife Countri The Woman and The Pilgrim's Progress of moral wisdom. This period was a witness of a news that become a merchandise and a beginning of a period when the essay become a part of art form and the start of textual criticism.

The dates for Restoration literature are vary considerably from genre to genre, so the drama form in the period of Restoration can last until 1700 and the poetry exist only until 1666. Main Authors

John Driden was an English poet, literary critic, translator and drama writer who in 1668 became the first English poet-laureate.
William Congreve was an English dramar and poet writer of the Restoration period. He got famous by the the smart, satirical dialogue and the impact on the comedy style during the Restoration period.
Aphra Behn was born on 14th december 1640, she was the British drama writer, poet, translator and fiction of the era in the period of Restoration. Aphra was the first English woman who actually lived from what she wrote, also she changed cultural barriers and she was represented as a model for later generations of female authors.
Anne Finch, Countess of Winchilsea was born in April 1661 and died in August 5, 1720. She was a poet in the period of Restoration. Some of her works also refer to other authors of that time, such as Aphra Behn and Katherine Phillips.

French Literature

French literature of the 17th century was during the Great French village, that the reign of Henry IV of France, Regency of Marie de Medici, Louis XIII of France, Regency of Anne of Austria (and civil war, known as the Fronde) and the reign written by Louis XIV of France. Literature of this period is often equated with a long reign of Louis XIV classicism, in which France took Europe into political and cultural development. Moreover, the beginning of the seventeenth century in French was represented as characteristical stop from the legacy of the war and native religious violence that interrupted the last forty years of the sixteenth century.

In order to achieve this delay, Henri IV was transferred from Protestantism to Catholicism in 1595, and few years later he declared Nantes’s edict which is a imperial ordinance which made a limitation on religious tolerance in the land of Huguenots, called French Protestants. The internal policies of France was not responsible for vanishing the religious controversy. In year 1610, Henri IV was killed by a Catholic fanatic, however, the peace that he made lasted until Louis XIV recall the edict in year 1685 and made French protestants to move abroad or proselyte.

In the period from 1610 and 1685, the culture across the country was influenced by the recognizable form of French absolutism. Until this day, the royal patronage of art is still organized around the academies, thanks to the descendants. One of considerable effects on the literary culture had the foundation of the Academie Francaise, which was arranged by Cardinal Richelieu in year 1630 in order to restore standards of the use and rhetoric in the language. Shortly it got an important role in forming literary French and the drama French form, however, his growth in fame was exacerbated by the increasing refinement of rhetoric that was defended in court and between the respected elites in France since the end of the XVI century.

Main Authors

Pierre Corneille a French tragedian who was born on 6 June 1606, died on 1 October. One of the greatest French dramatists of the 17th Century together with Molière and Racine.

Jean-Baptiste Poquelin or Molière, was born on 15 January 1622 and died on 17 February 1673. Moliere was a French drama writer and actor and one of the greatest masters of comedy in Western literature. His most famous works are The Misanthrope, The School for Wives, Tartuffe, The Miser, The Imaginary Invalid, and The Bourgeois Gentleman.

Jean Racine or Jean-Baptiste Racine, was born on 22 December 1639 and died on 21 April 1699, a French dramatist who was in a group of the three great drama writers of 17th Century of France. He was an important person in the Western tradition. Most of his works were genre of tragedy, such as Phèdre, Andromaque, and Athalie, however he wrote one comedy, Les Plaideurs, and a muted tragedy, Esther, for the young.

Jean de La Fontaine the most broadly read French poets of the 17th Century, who was born on 8 July 1621 and died on 13 April 1695. He got famous from his works, but the most popular was Fables.

Charles Perrault a French author and member of the Académie Française, was born on 12 January 1628 and died on 16 May 1703. He was a foundator of a modern literary genre, called the fairy tale. Some of his best tales are Le Petit Chaperon Rouge (Little Red Riding Hood), Cendrillon (Cinderella), Le Chat Botté (Puss in Boots), La Belle au bois Dormant (The Sleeping Beauty), and Barbe Bleue (Bluebeard).
